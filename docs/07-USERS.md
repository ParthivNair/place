# Users — Personas, Frequency & the Everyday Layer

**In one breath:** Place's user is the casual frontcountry recreator, not the gear-head — a base that is [record-large (183.2M Americans, 59% of everyone 6+)](https://www.outdoorsportswire.com/outdoor-recreation-participation-reaches-record-183-2-million-americans-according-to-new-outdoor-industry-association-report/) and getting structurally *more* casual, and whose modal query is mundane: "Trail" → Springville Trailhead, and "Beer" → Steeplejack — the first as a query, the second answered in context by the "nearby after" module (§4), never as a top-level surface. This document commits to the split that resolves the canon's tension: **everyday queries are hypothesized to be the majority of sessions (launch telemetry will confirm) and the retention driver; condition-gated moments are the differentiation and the moat** — and both are served from one graph, because a Tuesday Forest Park card rides the same seasonal priors and light conditions (mud after rain, daylight left, crowd) as a larch-season card. It names five personas with hypothesized weights, takes the founder's young-first adoption hypothesis seriously *and* corrects it with the evidence (older users are the fastest-growing outdoor cohort and the likeliest authors of the richest trip reports), refines the user stories the product is built around, and resolves the beer question without breaking [00-THESIS.md](00-THESIS.md): breweries enter the graph as **pairing nodes** on PAIRS_WITH edges (moat M5; the untimed generalization of the thesis's PAIRS_WITH_IN_WINDOW), never as a standalone commercial vertical. Persona weights and unsupported quantities are labeled hypotheses, to be replaced by launch telemetry.

Vocabulary per [00-THESIS.md](00-THESIS.md); feed mechanics per [02-PRODUCT.md](02-PRODUCT.md); phases and gates per [05-ROADMAP.md](05-ROADMAP.md).

## 1. The frequency/differentiation split, named and resolved

The canon's worked examples cluster on condition-gated niches — larch season, minus tides, flow bands — because those are where the moat lives. But the founding inversion in [00-THESIS.md](00-THESIS.md) §1 was never only about exotica. The original mental model is high-frequency and boring: type "Trail," get Springville Trailhead, Pittock Mansion Trailhead, Wildwood Trailhead; type "Beer," get Steeplejack, Breakside, McMenamins. The research says the boring model is where the users are — a user-base fact; whether it is also the session majority is a hypothesis telemetry will confirm (§3).

**The evidence for frequency.** Day hiking is America's biggest outdoor activity at [63.4M participants — one in five Americans 6+](https://material-civet.files.svdcdn.com/production/images/documents/2025-OIA_Participation_Trends_Full_Report_2025-12-15-211912_fchj.pdf) — and only [26.2% of hikers hit the 13-hikes/year core threshold](https://material-civet.files.svdcdn.com/production/images/documents/2025-OIA_Participation_Trends_Full_Report_2025-12-15-211912_fchj.pdf): roughly three in four hike about once a month or less. The whole base is shifting that way — [average outings per participant fell from 87.4 (2012) to 62.5 (2023)](https://material-civet.files.svdcdn.com/production/images/documents/2025-OIA_Participation_Trends_Full_Report_2025-12-15-211912_fchj.pdf), which the Outdoor Foundation itself calls a transition to a casual participant base. Locally the pyramid is steeper: [71.8% of Oregonians walk local trails (149M occasions/year) while only 17.8% ever backpack (6.2M occasions)](https://www.oregon.gov/oprd/PRP/Documents/SCORP-2023-Oregon-Resident-Outdoor-Recreation-Survey-Report.pdf) — frontcountry occasions outnumber backpacking roughly 24:1, in the most outdoorsy state Place will ever launch in. And demand concentrates on easy, road-accessible places: [local parks are the most popular recreation venues nationally](https://material-civet.files.svdcdn.com/production/images/documents/2025-OIA_Participation_Trends_Full_Report_2025-12-15-211912_fchj.pdf), and [Multnomah Falls alone draws 2M+ visitors a year](https://www.fs.usda.gov/r06/columbiarivergorge/recreation/multnomah-falls) — twice the [~1M annual visits to all of Forest Park and Marquam combined](https://giveguide.org/nonprofits/forest-park-conservancy).

**The evidence for differentiation.** Everyday queries are contested ground — "trailhead near me" is a Google Maps query ([~800M "near me" searches/month in the US; 76% lead to a visit within a day](https://www.brightlocal.com/resources/local-seo-statistics/)) and a solved AllTrails SEO page. What nobody answers is the conditional form, and the launch market proves the pain is real: [the PNW's #1 outdoor site is literally rationed by a government timed-entry permit for over three months a year](https://www.recreation.gov/timed-entry/10089144). Condition magic is the r/Portland post, the word-of-mouth, and moats M1–M4. Nobody screenshots "here are three trailheads"; everybody screenshots "swimmable right now at 980 cfs."

**The resolution, committed:** the split is a **claims-coverage and feed-composition decision, not a redesign**. The graph already handles both, because *there is no such thing as an unconditional outdoor experience* — the mundane Tuesday trailhead card rides seasonal priors plus light conditions: mud after 72-hour rain, daylight remaining against typical duration, crowd likelihood. The everyday layer is condition-*lite*, not condition-free. Concretely:

- **Feed composition rule:** an everyday-reliable base layer (nearby trailheads, walks, viewpoints with dense affordance claims and light condition annotation) is always rankable, with condition-magic cards ranked *on top* when their windows fire. The ≥60% live-reason quality gate in [02-PRODUCT.md](02-PRODUCT.md) is met honestly by counting mud state (NWS 72-h precip) and daylight remaining (ephemeris) as live reasons — both feed-sourced. The crowd prior is a prior, not a live reason: it annotates the card but does not count toward the 60% until parking-full verification exhaust ([02-PRODUCT.md](02-PRODUCT.md) §5.3) makes it live.
- **Generic-intent browse ships alongside the temporal feed:** typing an activity verb ("trail," "swim," "walk") returns the everyday ranking for that verb. At launch scale this is a filter on the feed, not a search engine — NL search stays deferred per [02-PRODUCT.md](02-PRODUCT.md) §6.
- **Frequency retains, magic converts.** Everyday utility earns the weekly habit and the save; condition moments earn the share and the verification story. Retention math needs the first; the moat metric needs the second; the Sunday push welds them together.

*Losing alternative:* a condition-magic-only product — a few spectacular sessions a year, no weekly ritual, and a feed that is empty most Tuesdays. Also losing: an everyday-only product — that is AllTrails with worse SEO.

## 2. Characteristic breakdown: five personas

What the research says about the base before the personas: it is newer than the stereotype ([over half of participants have under 10 years of experience](https://www.outdoorsportswire.com/outdoor-recreation-participation-reaches-record-183-2-million-americans-according-to-new-outdoor-industry-association-report/)), near gender parity in participation ([women hit a record 53.4% rate in 2025](https://material-civet.files.svdcdn.com/production/images/documents/2025-OIA_Participation_Trends_Full_Report_2025-12-15-211912_fchj.pdf) though still [~44% of core participants](https://material-civet.files.svdcdn.com/production/images/documents/2025-OIA_Participation_Trends_Full_Report_2025-12-15-211912_fchj.pdf)), income-representative of America ([participants' income distribution matches the general population](https://material-civet.files.svdcdn.com/production/images/documents/2025-OIA_Participation_Trends_Full_Report_2025-12-15-211912_fchj.pdf)), and mostly free-riding on incumbent apps — AllTrails has [80M+ registered users](https://techcrunch.com/2025/05/12/alltrails-debuts-a-80-year-membership-that-includes-ai-powered-smart-routes/) against [1M paid in 2021](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/alltrails-celebrates-1-million-paid-subscribers-301214556.html), so a paid share on the order of 3–5% is a reasonable [inference](https://www.revenuecat.com/blog/growth/alltrails-product-channel/); the category's users overwhelmingly do not pay for discovery. Portland matches the profile that over-participates: [Oregon's above-average participators are high-income, under-60, and urban](https://www.oregon.gov/oprd/PRP/Documents/SCORP-2023-Oregon-Resident-Outdoor-Recreation-Survey-Report.pdf), and [36.9% of Portlanders are 25–44](https://www.neilsberg.com/insights/portland-or-population-by-age/).

**All persona weights below are hypotheses to validate with launch telemetry.** The research supports the *ordering* (casual majority, core minority); it contains no measurement of Place's actual mix, and we will not pretend otherwise.

### P1 — The casual urban defaulter (hypothesis: ~40% of early users)
**25–34 (a hypothesis, inside Portland's dominant 25–44 band — [36.9% of the city](https://www.neilsberg.com/insights/portland-or-population-by-age/)), casual (once a month or less), the majority persona.** The [~74% of hikers below the core threshold](https://material-civet.files.svdcdn.com/production/images/documents/2025-OIA_Participation_Trends_Full_Report_2025-12-15-211912_fchj.pdf). Discovers via Instagram/TikTok ([80%/62% penetration at 18–29](https://www.pewresearch.org/internet/fact-sheet/social-media/)) and Google Maps "near me." Time budget: 2–3 hours, decided same-day. Frequently a dog — [60.6% of Portland-area residents own a pet](https://www.usnews.com/insurance/pet-insurance/top-dog-loving-cities), and Portland is #1 nationally for dog-park access — so the dog flag is first-class, not an edge case. Payment propensity: near zero; reads, rarely writes ([only ~29% of consumers wrote any review last year](https://www.brightlocal.com/research/local-consumer-review-survey-2025/)). Types: **"Trail." "Beer." "swim." "something outside today."** This persona is why the everyday layer exists and why onboarding is a no-login tool.

### P2 — The weekend planner (hypothesis: ~25%)
**30–45, regular (monthly-plus), often kids and/or a dog.** The Thursday-evening user [02-PRODUCT.md](02-PRODUCT.md) was designed for. Plans 48–72 hours out, optimizes against nap schedules, parking, and weather. Time budget: a half-day window, known in advance. The crowd question is existential for them — the [Multnomah timed-entry permit](https://www.recreation.gov/timed-entry/10089144) is their pain made policy. Highest payment propensity of any persona: what converts in this category is [trip-assurance utility at $36–80/year](https://techcrunch.com/2025/05/12/alltrails-debuts-a-80-year-membership-that-includes-ai-powered-smart-routes/), sold to people already committed to an outing — which is exactly this persona's state every Thursday. Types: "easy hike with kids Saturday morning," "which trailhead will have parking at 10."

### P3 — The social-search native (hypothesis: ~15%)
**18–24, casual, discovery-first.** Searches [Instagram (67%) and TikTok (62%) at rates exceeding Google (61%)](https://www.forbes.com/sites/johnkoetsier/2024/03/11/genz-dumping-google-for-tiktok-instagram-as-social-search-wins/) for inspiration queries — though the TikTok-over-Google narrative is softening, and Google keeps the logistics query. [87% seek reviews but trust video and social proof](https://www.brightlocal.com/research/local-consumer-review-survey-2025/); contributes little text. Time budget: spontaneous, group-driven, aesthetic-driven. Payment propensity: zero. Growth signal: Gen Z is [Strava's fastest-growing demographic, with hiking clubs up 5.8x in 2025](https://press.strava.com/articles/strava-releases-12th-annual-year-in-sport-trend-report-2025). Types: "that waterfall from the video," "golden hour viewpoint." They arrive via the screenshot of a condition card — this persona is who the *differentiation* layer acquires.

### P4 — The core enthusiast / verifier (hypothesis: ~10%, disproportionately valuable)
**28–60, core (weekly-plus), condition-literate.** The [minority tier of the base](https://material-civet.files.svdcdn.com/production/images/documents/2025-OIA_Participation_Trends_Full_Report_2025-12-15-211912_fchj.pdf) that already reads gauges and writes the trip reports the extraction pipeline eats — WTA's corpus is [~282,000 reports deep](https://www.wta.org/go-outside/trip-reports). [Core participants spend ~30% more on gear](https://material-civet.files.svdcdn.com/production/images/documents/2025-OIA_Participation_Trends_Full_Report_2025-12-15-211912_fchj.pdf) and are the plausible premium buyers, but their real value is ground truth: the 10 named power verifiers in [05-ROADMAP.md](05-ROADMAP.md) Phase 3 come from this persona, recruited by provenance credit, not badges. Types: "Clackamas flow right now," "larch status Grassy Knoll," — and, crucially, *answers*: confirm / deny / changed.

### P5 — The weekday walker (hypothesis: ~10% early, the structural growth wave)
**55+, casual-to-regular, time-rich.** The fastest-growing outdoor cohort in America — [65+ participation went from 25.7% (2016) to 41.6% (2025)](https://material-civet.files.svdcdn.com/production/images/documents/2025-OIA_Participation_Trends_Full_Report_2025-12-15-211912_fchj.pdf), now a larger cohort than 18–24, 45–54, 55–64, or 6–12. Not tech-avoidant: [90% smartphone ownership at 50–64, 78% at 65+](https://www.pewresearch.org/internet/fact-sheet/mobile/), and adults 50+ [use ~10 apps a quarter](https://www.aarp.org/pri/topics/technology/internet-media-devices/2026-technology-trends-older-adults/). The gate is design and trust: [3 in 5 say tech isn't designed with their age in mind, and privacy is the #1 adoption barrier](https://www.aarp.org/pri/topics/technology/internet-media-devices/2026-technology-trends-older-adults/). Time budget: weekday mornings — off-peak demand that spreads load for free. Types: "flat paved 2-mile walk," "accessible waterfall, weekday."

### The adoption-wave hypothesis, examined honestly

The founder's hypothesis — adoption starts young and expands older as usability eases — is **supported for the app layer and inverted for the participation and content layers**. Supported: AllTrails' audience is roughly [62% Millennial + Gen Z](https://www.similarweb.com/website/alltrails.com/) (third-party data; AllTrails publishes no demographics), and Gen Z is [Strava's fastest-growing cohort](https://press.strava.com/articles/strava-releases-12th-annual-year-in-sport-trend-report-2025). Acquire young — P1 and P3 are the launch wave, and the r/Portland-and-screenshot GTM targets them correctly.

But the counter-nuance is load-bearing: **the corpus authors and the best verifier candidates skew older.** [Boomers and Gen X out-logged Millennials and Gen Z in both mileage and KOM/QOM crowns on Strava in 2024](https://press.strava.com/articles/strava-releases-year-in-sport-trend-report); ["retired" was the single largest occupation among 2025 PCT hikers (12.2%)](https://www.halfwayanywhere.com/trails/pacific-crest-trail/pct-hiker-survey-2025/) and [retirees plus job-quitters were over half of AT survey respondents](https://thetrek.co/appalachian-trail/the-2025-appalachian-trail-thru-hiker-survey-general-information-part-1/). The people with weekday time to hike and write detailed condition reports are disproportionately older — and the graph is precisely the machine that transfers their knowledge to the young majority: a retiree's Tuesday verification prices Saturday's card for a 27-year-old who will never read a forum. The claim that retirees verify disproportionately on weekdays is a labeled **inference** (no dataset cross-tabs trail use by age and day-of-week); the design implication stands anyway, because the fix — readable type, plain language, a privacy posture worth trusting — costs little and per the [usability literature](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12350549/) is a set of fixable design choices, not a market limit. Wave 2 is not "older users someday"; it is a Phase 6-era usability pass ([05-ROADMAP.md](05-ROADMAP.md)) for a cohort growing faster than the young one right now.

## 4. The beer question, resolved without breaking canon

[00-THESIS.md](00-THESIS.md) §6 commits Place to unowned, unbookable experiences — commercial POIs are Google's monetized turf, and a brewery-discovery app is a Yelp clone with worse coverage. But the founder's "Beer" query is real behavior, and in Portland it is *codified* behavior: the post-hike brewery is a whole publishing genre ([Beer Hiking Pacific Northwest, born from the Beers at the Bottom blog](https://www.beeradvocate.com/articles/17393/10-questions-with-the-authors-of-beer-hiking-pacific-northwest/), plus trail-to-tavern guides that [pair Lower Macleay with specific breweries](https://www.toadandco.com/blogs/blog/the-best-trail-to-tavern-pairings-near-portland)), against a supply of [~70 in-city breweries](https://www.crresearch.com/blog/which-cities-have-most-craft-breweries/) — a [contracting market (~30 Oregon closures in 2025)](https://www.northwestbeerguide.com/home/2026-oregonbrewersalliance-5percent) whose survivors are motivated foot-traffic partners.

**The commitment: breweries and their kin enter the graph as PAIRING nodes, never as primary objects.** A pairing node is the après layer — brewery, diner, ice-cream stand, soaking pool — attached to outdoor experiences via **PAIRS_WITH edges (moat M5** — PAIRS_WITH_IN_WINDOW in [00-THESIS.md](00-THESIS.md), named here without the window because the module fires post-outing**)**, surfaced as a **"nearby after" module** on place pages and after the "I'm going" tap — the only trip surface that exists at launch, Trip Builder being deferred per [02-PRODUCT.md](02-PRODUCT.md) §6. The "Beer" want resolves *through* the outdoor graph, in context: Steeplejack, Breakside, and McMenamins appear because they pair with the trailheads you use, not because Place indexes taprooms or serves "Beer" as a query. No brewery page, no brewery reviews, no brewery feed — the moment a commercial POI is a destination rather than an edge, we are competing with Google on Google's key.

The strategic asymmetry that makes this better than a deferral: **an "after this" tap feeds the graph.** Unlike the Trip Builder — deferred in [02-PRODUCT.md](02-PRODUCT.md) §6 precisely because it exercises the graph without feeding it — every tap on a "nearby after" suggestion writes a PAIRS_WITH observation: this trailhead, this brewery, this cohort, this season. M5's pairing edges, which [00-THESIS.md](00-THESIS.md) marks "later, learned from trip acceptances and co-visits," start accumulating from a one-module surface years before the Trip Builder renders them. Pairing data earns its place at launch; trip assembly still waits.
